Datasets

AI4Bharat: IndicVoices

IndicVoices is an extensive dataset of natural and spontaneous speech, curated by AI4Bharat. This dataset includes a total of 7,348 hours of audio, encompassing read (9%), extempore (74%), and conversational (17%) speech. The recordings feature 16,237 speakers from 145 districts across India, covering 22 languages. Of the total 7,348 hours, 1,639 hours have been transcribed, with a median of 73 hours transcribed per language.

Linguistic Data Consortium for Indian Languages (LDC-IL)

The Raw Speech Corpus is part of the Linguistic Data Consortium for Indian Languages (LDC-IL) collection. It comprises hours of speech data, featuring audio segments. This corpus includes recordings from approximately 100 speakers (female and male) and covers various domains such as contemporary text (news), creative text, sentences, and command words. The data is available in high-quality WAV format and is crucial for developing speech recognition and natural language processing tools for the Indian language.

IIT Mandi Indian languages - Local

We use locally collected data. speech samples in 8 Indian languages. This dataset is for Language Identification mainly, only one language is present in each sample, split according to the languages spoken.
